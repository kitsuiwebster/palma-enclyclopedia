About Palma-1.0 Dataset

PalmTraits 1.0 is a comprehensive dataset on the world's palms, enriched with information from GBIF, iNaturalist, and POWO. It documents the diversity, distribution, and ecological characteristics of palms, an essential botanical family in tropical and subtropical ecosystems.

Dataset Structure

Detailed exploration of variables and their distribution in PalmTraits 1.0

Palma-1.0 contains comprehensive data on over 2,500 palm species across 181 genera. The dataset combines morphological traits, taxonomic classification, geographic distribution, and detailed fruit characteristics. This database has been enhanced with information from GBIF, iNaturalist, and POWO (Plants of the World Online).

Methodology

Approach used for collecting and analyzing palm data

1

Data Collection

Primary data comes from the PalmTraits 1.0 dataset, a compilation of scientific research on world palms. This information was enriched with API queries to GBIF, iNaturalist, and POWO platforms to obtain updated occurrence and taxonomic data.

2

Taxonomic Harmonization

We used the reference taxonomy from Plants of the World Online (POWO) as a standard to harmonize species names and resolve synonymies. This process eliminated duplications and ensured the consistency of taxonomic data.

3

Validation and Cleaning

The collected data was subjected to a rigorous validation process to identify and correct outliers, inconsistencies, or missing values. Records with major inconsistencies were manually verified using scientific literature.

4

Geospatial Enrichment

Occurrence data was enriched with environmental information extracted from global geospatial layers, including bioclimatic, topographic, and land cover variables, to better characterize the ecological niches of species.

5

Analysis and Visualization

Statistical methods and data visualization were applied to explore distribution patterns, phylogenetic relationships, and functional traits of palms. The results were compiled in this interactive interface to facilitate data exploration.
